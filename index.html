Market Snapshot

Phantom Aria (GD04) hit shelves five days ago and is already showing the new chase pattern: Destiny Gundam LR++ at $755 and Zeong LR++ at $452 immediately took the top of the GD04 leaderboard, while the booster box sits at $104 (just above $99 MSRP), a typical fresh-release equilibrium. The interesting story is older product. GD01 Newtype Rising boxes are running at $263, more than 2.6x MSRP, and case prices are at $2,617, suggesting distributor-level dryup as Bandai's reprint cadence hasn't caught the secondary market. GD03 Steel Requiem boxes, by contrast, are below MSRP at $91.88 three months after release, hinting at oversupply or weak meta absorption. GD02 Dual Impact is mid-pack at $126. Promo cards (Regional Championship Amuro Ray $1,100, Newtype Challenge Mission promos $400-$736) remain the highest-priced singles outside Edition Beta.

Meta layer: 103 decks aggregated from the last 3 real Gundam tournaments on Limitless TCG (Eagle's Nest GD04 4/23, MSA Steel Requiem Royal Battle 4/11, Eagle's Nest S4 Finals 4/16). The competitive picture is stable: GD01 staples (A Show of Resolve, Overflowing Affection) plus the Wing/UC starter staples (Gundam ST01-001, Amuro Ray ST01-010) form the core of nearly every top-8 deck. GD02 added one true staple (Rick Dias GD02-079), GD03 added Gundam Barbatos Adapt and Gundam NT-1, GD04 hasn't added a sub-30% play rate card to the staple pool yet beyond chase LR++.
